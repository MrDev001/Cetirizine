Cetirizine

Second-generation antihistamine (piperazine derivative); carboxylic acid metabolite of hydroxyzine.

FDA Approval: 1995 (Rx), OTC status in 2007

Chemical Formula: C₂₁H₂₅ClN₂O₃•2HCl
Molecular Weight: 461.82

Mechanism of Action (MOA)

  • Selective peripheral H1-receptor antagonist
  • Inhibits histamine effects in smooth muscles, endothelium, immune & GI cells
  • Minimal CNS penetration → low sedation
  • Reduces vascular permeability, histamine-induced bronchospasm
  • Anti-inflammatory: inhibits eosinophil & neutrophil migration

Indications

  • Seasonal rhinitis and conjunctivitis
  • Perennial allergic rhinitis
  • Pruritis and urticaria of allergic origin
  • Chronic idiopathic urticaria
  • Acute urticaria (IV form)
  • Allergic conjunctivitis (ophthalmic form)

Dosage

For Oral Solution:

Children 6 months to 2 years

For Perennial Allergic Rhinitis & Chronic Urticaria:
2.5 mg (1/2 teaspoon) once daily

Children 2 to 5 years

2.5 mg (1/2 teaspoon) once daily

Children 6 years and above

2 teaspoons once daily or 1 teaspoon twice daily

For Tablets:

Children over 12 years and adults

One tablet daily or as directed by the physician

Adverse Drug Reactions (ADRs)

  • Common: Somnolence, fatigue, dry mouth, dizziness, pharyngitis
  • Pediatrics: Headache, abdominal pain (dose-related)
  • Ophthalmic Form: Hyperemia, instillation pain
  • Rare: Hepatitis, liver enzyme elevation, anaphylaxis, thrombocytopenia, glomerulonephritis

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

  • Absorption: Rapid GI absorption; peak in 1 hr; onset: 20–60 min
  • Distribution: 93% plasma protein-bound
  • Metabolism: Minimal; not via CYP450; P-gp substrate
  • Excretion: Renal; t½ ≈ 8.3 hrs

Interactions

Drug-Drug:

  • ↑ Sedation with alcohol, opioids, benzodiazepines
  • ↓ Gabapentin exposure, ↑ CNS depression
  • ↓ Pitolisant efficacy (avoid use together)
  • Verapamil & Erdafitinib ↑ cetirizine CNS levels (P-gp inhibition)

Drug-Food:

  • Food delays peak but does not reduce overall absorption

Drug-Disease:

  • Use with caution in hepatic/renal impairment

Contraindications

  • No contraindication has been declared to date.

Special Population Precautions:

  • Cetirizine does not potentiate alcohol effects at 0.08% blood concentration, but caution is still required.

Pregnancy and Lactation

  • Animal studies show no specific malformations.
  • Should not be used during organogenesis.
  • Avoid during lactation.

Storage

For Solution:

  • Do not store above 25°C.
  • Protect from light.
  • Keep medicine out of the reach of children.

For Tablets:

  • Do not store above 30°C.
  • Protect from light.
  • Keep medicine out of the reach of children.
